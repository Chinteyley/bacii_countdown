# Bac II Countdown — Product Context

## Register
brand

## Users
Cambodian Grade 12 students preparing for the Bac II national exam. They are
stressed, time-aware, and check this page semi-frequently — typically once or
twice a day, often late evening. ~80%+ of visits are mobile, the rest tablet,
with occasional laptop. Many balance studying with chores, side jobs, or family
responsibilities. They are 17–19 years old.

## Product Purpose
A single-page countdown to the Bac II exam (10 August 2026, 07:00 ICT). It is
**not** a study tool. It is one number on a black screen that they can return
to. The site does emotional work by being almost-nothing — a steady, industrial,
minimal presence. No copy. No coaching. No emoji. Just the day count.

## Brand & Tone
- Industrial. Minimal. Achromatic.
- Calm via emptiness rather than warmth.
- Inspired by the Nothing OS aesthetic: monochrome, dot-matrix display, corner
  meta-information, one red Glyph-style indicator.
- Anti-product. Reads like a system widget, not a website.

## Anti-references
- Hustle/grindset countdowns (flame emoji, "X days to crush it").
- Generic SaaS hero pages with rotating gradient text.
- Gamified habit trackers and exam-prep apps.
- Newspaper-grade alarmism ("DAY X OF Y!!!").
- Glassmorphism, neon, the AI-slop hero-metric template.
- Warm "supportive" copy, encouragement banners, motivational quotes.

## Strategic Principles
1. **The day count is the only thing that has to be perfect.** Everything else
   exists in the corners.
2. **Mobile is the canonical surface.** Tablet and desktop are expansions of the
   mobile vertical.
3. **No copy if a number can do the job.** Time, target date, and "days" are the
   only words on screen.
4. **Less is the brand.** Every removed element is a feature.
5. **Stillness is okay.** Nothing moves except the day rollover and the seconds
   ticker.
